A One-Shot Deal letter for NYC cash assistance should be a formal letter to the NYC Human Resources Administration (HRA) explaining your urgent need for financial assistance due to unforeseen circumstances, such as rent arrears. You must clearly state the specific problem (e.g., eviction threat), the amount of money needed, why you are unable to pay, and your plan for future rent payments, often requiring a third party to agree to help. Attach all necessary documents to support your claim, such as your lease, proof of income, and a rent statement from your landlord. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

Important tips for your application 

• Be honest and direct: Clearly state your situation and the amount you need. 
• Be proactive: Follow up with your caseworker and HRA frequently. 
• Submit all required documents: Provide a lease, proof of income, and a landlord's statement of arrears. You may also need to provide proof of identity for all household members. 
• Explain future payment plan: You will need to show how you will afford future rent payments, often by getting a third party to agree to help. 
• Consider other resources: If you have trouble with the process, reach out to your City Council member for assistance. 
• Expect a delay: It may take 30-45 days to get a decision after submitting all documents. [1, 2, 3, 4, 9, 10, 11, 12]
